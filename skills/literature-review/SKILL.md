# Literature Review

Use this skill to synthesize known sources, plan source acquisition, identify
gaps, and connect literature to research questions without losing provenance.

## Allowed Mutation Mode

Artifact mutation only for review notes, source manifests, and synthesis
artifacts when authorized. Do not rewrite manuscript prose unless routed to
paper planning or LaTeX authoring.

## Artifact Inputs

- Research brief or scoped question
- Source manifest
- Bibliography state
- Notes or excerpts supplied by the user
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria when available

## Workflow

1. Confirm the review purpose: background, related work, systematic screen,
   positioning, or claim support.
2. Inventory sources and label missing metadata.
3. Group sources by problem, method, result, limitation, or disagreement.
4. Separate summary from interpretation.
5. Produce synthesis notes and source gaps that can feed paper planning.

## Artifact Outputs

- Literature synthesis notes
- Source gap list
- Candidate claim support map
- Inclusion and exclusion decision notes

## Source Support

Every substantive summary should point to a source manifest entry,
bibliography key, or user-provided excerpt. Flag claims that depend on unread,
unavailable, or low-confidence sources. Do not fabricate citations or quote
text that was not supplied.

## Human Checkpoints

The human researcher approves source inclusion, exclusion, interpretation, and
the level of confidence before synthesis becomes manuscript argumentation.
